Guide

Find the right muscle

Your pelvic floor muscles are the ones you'd use to stop urine mid-flow or hold in gas. Try it once while on the toilet to locate them — but don't make a habit of exercising while urinating.

When you squeeze, you should feel a gentle lift inside. Your belly, thighs and glutes should stay relaxed.

Technique

  1. Sit, lie down, or stand comfortably. Breathe normally.
  2. Squeeze and lift the pelvic floor when the app says so.
  3. Release fully during rest — the relaxation is as important as the squeeze.
  4. Don't hold your breath, clench your abs, or tighten your buttocks.

Tips

  • Consistency beats intensity — 3 short sessions a day works best.
  • Attach it to a habit: after brushing teeth, during a commute, before bed.
  • Expect noticeable results in 4–6 weeks of daily practice.
  • Nobody can tell you're exercising — do it anywhere.

Note: This app is for general fitness. If you have pain, are pregnant, or recently had surgery, consult a doctor or pelvic health physiotherapist first.
