# 01 · Market Research

Analysis of leading Kegel / pelvic-floor apps to inform a superior, original product.
(Research snapshot: July 2026.)

## Competitive landscape

### App-only trainers (no hardware)

| App | Model | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Kegel Trainer** (Jsdev, iOS/Android) | Freemium; one-time unlock or small subscription | Simple session timers, 10+ levels, reminders; huge install base | Dated UI; no personalization; no education depth; English-only |
| **Squeezy** (NHS-endorsed, UK) | Paid app (~£2.99), + Squeezy for Men variant | Clinical credibility (chartered physiotherapists); clinician-connect model | Utilitarian design; no gamification; UK-centric; minimal motivation loop |
| **Stamena** (men) | Freemium + IAP | Goal-specific (stamina); progressive levels | Single-purpose; explicit framing limits mainstream appeal |
| **Pelvic Floor Pro** | $7.99/wk or $89.99/yr | Medically designed plans, education | Aggressive pricing; woman-focused only |
| **Connect Pelvic Floor Fitness** | $15/mo or $180/yr | Video-led coaching | Expensive; heavy content, not habit-sized |
| **Easy Kegel / Kehel / Daily Kegel** | Free/ads or cheap IAP | Simplicity | No science, no progression logic, ad-cluttered |

### Hardware + app (premium end)

| Product | Price | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Perifit / Perifit Care+** | ~$149 device | Real-time biofeedback games — best-in-class gamification; proves users love *game-play*, not clinical checklists |
| **Elvie Trainer** | ~$200 device | Premium brand, LED/app feedback; higher price, mixed reviews vs Perifit |
| **kGoal, femfit** | $100–250 | Niche clinical/consumer crossovers |

Hardware apps matter because they set the **engagement bar**: real-time feedback,
games, visible progress. An app-only product must replicate that *feeling* through
animation, sound, haptics, streaks, and a visible score.

## Key takeaways → product decisions

1. **No leader in India.** Every major app is US/UK-centric, English-only, priced in
   USD/GBP. **12 Indian languages + INR store pricing is an open moat.**
2. **Clinical credibility sells** (Squeezy's NHS badge). → Physio-reviewed protocol,
   evidence citations in Learn section, "reviewed by" credit.
3. **Gamification drives retention** (Perifit). → Streaks, XP, badges, a signature
   breathing-circle session player that feels like biofeedback (doc 06).
4. **Onboarding is the weakest link everywhere** — most apps dump users on a timer
   list. → Personalized quiz → generated plan → first session within 3 minutes
   (docs 02, 05).
5. **Discretion matters.** Users are embarrassed by this category. → Discreet app
   icon option, app lock, private-by-default, no social pressure.
6. **Both genders, separate journeys.** Squeezy ships *two apps*; we ship one app
   with gender-branched onboarding, content, and visuals — half the maintenance,
   twice the market (postpartum recovery + prostate health + continence + sexual
   wellness).
7. **Pricing reality check.** Global comparables land at ₹500–800/mo equivalent.
   ₹999/mo is premium-end but viable **if** the ₹2,999 lifetime plan is the hero
   offer (strong anchor, one-time payment suits Indian buying habits). Recommend a
   7-day free trial on monthly and intro pricing experiments (doc 08).
8. **Common user complaints to avoid** (from store reviews across category):
   paywall before any value; lost progress after reinstall; notification spam;
   no way to adjust difficulty; unclear "am I doing it right?" → free tier gives a
   real daily session; cloud sync; notification frequency controls; difficulty
   adjust mid-plan; strong technique education with cues.

## Differentiation summary

**"The first pelvic-floor coach built for India"** — physio-grade program,
game-grade motivation, in your language, priced in rupees, discreet by design,
for men and women.
